### POST 5 — Andrea Fanelli | 2 days ago (OPENED IN DIALOG — FULL DETAILS)
**Author:** Andrea Fanelli (Rising Contributor)
**Reactions:** 0

**Full Text:**
Hi all, I'm 85 kg and mainly use my setup for wingfoiling and downwind runs with the wing, mostly on lakes. I consider myself an intermediate winger (still working on my tacks, but can gybe all day, switch feet, and ride swell comfortably). My current quiver is: ART V2 999 for 14–25mph and ART V2 939 for strong wind days (20-25mph). The 999 is my daily driver (I use it 90% of the time). After an initial adjustment period, I've really come to love the ART V2 wings and feel very comfortable on them. The challenge is that I live in Seattle, where the wind can often be quite light (8-14mph). Getting the 999 to take off in such light winds is exhausting and frustrating, so much pumping and I get gassed out fast. For those days, I also have a PNG 910B and a Spitfire 1180. However, I do not have as much fun on those foils. The spitfire feels hard to turn, while the PNG 910 turns nicely but feels quite slow. So I always end up using the 999 anyway, which makes the whole experience super frustrating because I struggle so much to get it to foil. I'm looking for a light-wind front wing (8–14 mph) that still feels fun and fast, similar to my ART V2 foils. I am mostly considering the Surge 1010 or the artV2 1099, but I worry they might not be enough for such light winds. Any recommendation? How do they compare in terms of turn ability and low end? I could also consider fireballs, but I am not sure if I am good enough for that. Other option is a PNG1200 v2 since I also want to get into dock starting, but I assume it will be comparable to the Spitfire as behavior (so not great at turning). Thanks!

**Equipment Mentioned:** ART V2 999, ART V2 939, PNG 910B, Spitfire 1180, Surge 1010, ART V2 1099, Fireball, PNG 1200 V2
**Rider Weight:** 85 kg
**Discipline:** Wing foiling, downwind (lakes)
**Location:** Seattle

**Comments:**
- **Leonardo Loppi** (1d ago): "Go for surge. Art V2 is not friendly in take off. 1180 the best for that, but as you said not that funny. If you can start 999 in light wind, go for Surge 950 and will find how easy it going on. As others says stab is very important too. Progressive for start early, skinny for go faster, surf for be more loose and manovrable" — 3 reactions
  - **Andrea Fanelli** reply: "you think 950 is enough for such light winds at my weight? I wish I could demo it in my area"
- **Sean Richardson** (2d ago, Edited): "I'm 85kg. Used to ride 1099 and 999v1, 939v2, 1030 spitfire, etc. 1030 was my latest light wind foil for winging with 6.5m and 94ltr 23" wide board, until I made the switch to Surges - 1010, 890, 830, etc. Surge 1010 is my light wind weapon, and the most fun I've had on a big wing. For winging, I would never go bigger - it has incredibly low take off speed - due to the foil profile - a couple of pumps in 10knots and I'm up and flying. However, I'm an advanced wing Foiler so I might get going more easily, but I can tell you it's definitely a step up from 999v2… if you want ultra light wind you might look at the larger Fireball - it's not too advanced, especially if you're just cruising around in light winds. Choice of foil for light wind will also depend on your board (ie super light construction, more downwind narrow style for easy take off)…. And the size of your largest wind wing. For turning, you will get a lot out of going shorter Fuse and small stab with your 1180, or any large foil for that matter. On my 1010 (and when I had the 1030), I use a silly short adv++ and ride progressive stab 275 - this set up loosens the big foils up considerably, but because you are riding a large front wing, there is still plenty of stability (usually it takes an initial 1-2 sessions to get used to the change in pitch stability, but after that you start to love how responsive pitch can feel on a large front foil)" — 2 reactions
