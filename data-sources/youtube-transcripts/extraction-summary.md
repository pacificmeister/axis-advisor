## Notable Insights Discovered

### Designer Insights (Adrian Roper)
- **Area vs Span:** Three 1000mm-span foils (ART V2 999 at 1024 area, Fireball 1000 at 773, Tempo 1020 at 650) all get up the same. Area comparison is meaningless with modern high-camber designs.
- **Camber:** Fireballs, Surges, and Tempos all use 3.7% camber (vs 2.5% on older Spitfires/Art Pros), which is the key to their low-speed lift and glide.
- **Uni-carbon breakthrough:** Replacing woven carbon with uni-directional on wing exteriors delivered 25% more stiffness — enables even higher aspect designs.
- **Straight median line:** All modern AXIS wings use straight median line (started with Spitfire), which means the center of lift stays consistent across speeds, allowing much smaller rear wings.
- **Surge 'gross mustache' wing tips:** The turn-down/turn-up design makes the foil feel narrower, roll easier, breach better, and aid re-entry from jumps — but adds drag and reduces top speed vs flat wings.

### Performance Benchmarks
- **Tempo dominance:** 9 out of top 20 riders in Maui race series were on AXIS Tempos
- **World record:** Niko Itan dock-start pumped 20.12km in 1 hour on the 1500 span pump wing
- **Surge vs Spitfire:** Luke (100kg) can surf the Surge 890 harder than the Spitfire 780 AND pump it 10x longer
- **PNG V2 1400:** Described as "easiest foil ever dock started" by experienced reviewer (Dominic Hoskins)
- **Fatty mast:** Bends only 11.7mm vs 38mm for aluminum — enables control of 1750+ span wings

### Sizing Guides Mentioned
- ART V2 999 → Surge 890 or 950
- ART V2 819 → Surge 830
- Spitfire 780 → Surge 830 (size up for more pump)
- PNG V2 1400 recommended for riders over 90kg; lighter riders should use 1300
- Surf Skinny rear wing sizing: 280 (lighter/Kai), 300 (mid/Luke at 100kg), 320 (heavy/Sven)
