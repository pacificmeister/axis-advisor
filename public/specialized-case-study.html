Executive Summary

Specialized Bicycle Components is the gold standard for anti-counterfeiting enforcement in the carbon fiber sporting goods industry. Over 15+ years, they built a comprehensive program culminating in the largest counterfeit bust in cycling history (December 2025).

Their approach — combining platform partnerships, legal warfare, test purchase campaigns, safety narratives, and factory raids — is directly applicable to AXIS's counterfeit foil problem. Both companies face the same threat: Chinese factories producing carbon fiber copies sold through AliExpress/Alibaba.

Timeline of Key Actions

2007–2010: Building the Foundation

  • Andrew Love hired at Specialized (now Global Brand Protection Manager)
  • 2010: Love identifies the "tsunami" of counterfeit cycling goods as Chinese e-commerce becomes visible in US searches
  • Specialized begins building a dedicated brand protection function

2016: Going Public

  • Launches public anti-counterfeit awareness campaign
  • Love publicly demonstrates counterfeit helmet crush tests — fake helmets fail "catastrophically" due to missing internal roll cage
  • Deploys 13-person Global Brand Protection team
  • Key messaging shift: Safety angle — "These aren't just knockoffs, they're dangerous"

2017: First Major Joint Operation with Alibaba

  • 2-year joint investigation with Alibaba culminates in major factory raid
  • Digital evidence from AliExpress → traced to physical manufacturing facility
  • Factory raided, operator jailed
  • Establishes the partnership model used for the next 8 years

2018–2019: US Legal Offensive

  • 2018: Lawsuit against 69 anonymous AliExpress sellers
  • 2019: Lawsuit against 43 more sellers (AliExpress + Wish.com)
  • US District Court, Southern Florida orders judgment in favor of Specialized
  • Statutory damages: up to $1 million per seller ($43M potential total)
  • Court ordered: domain seizures, PayPal fund transfers to Specialized, injunctions
  • Result: 19 of 38 stores shuttered (illustrating the whack-a-mole challenge)
Key Quote from Andrew Love (2019):
"Welcome to the frustrating world of brand protection. These are not legitimate bike shops or legitimate businesses. These are e-commerce counterfeit sellers."

2022: Systematic Three-Strike Campaign (Rouse Law Firm)

12-Month Results:
  • 1,020 test purchases completed
  • 638 counterfeit samples confirmed (63% hit rate)
  • 100 Taobao stores permanently banned
  • 188 stores received 1–2 strikes with penalties
  • 38 stores self-banned preemptively
  • Counterfeit purchase success rate dropped from 80% to 30%

This is the most granular data available showing that systematic enforcement works. The campaign was conducted by Rouse, a law firm specializing in Chinese IP enforcement.

March 2025: Largest Counterfeit Bust in Cycling History

Joint operation: Specialized + AliExpress + Chinese Police (Hangzhou)
  • 2 factory operations dismantled
  • 7 individuals arrested and prosecuted
  • $1.1M in counterfeit Specialized Tarmac SL8 road racing frames
  • Roval handlebars and wheels, seatposts, forks
  • 9,500 sticker sets (for branding counterfeits)
  • Also seized: fake Pinarello, Cannondale, Cervelo, Trek products
  • Total estimated street value: $1.6 million+
"The success of this largest counterfeit bust in the cycling industry highlights the profound impact of collaboration between brands like ours and tech-driven platforms like AliExpress. Chinese law enforcement and Alibaba deserve immense recognition for their integral role in achieving this milestone."
— Andrew Love, Global Brand Protection Manager, Specialized
"Safeguarding consumers and upholding brand trust are core to our platform's integrity. While we swiftly remove infringing listings from our marketplace, lasting impact requires dismantling physical operations through close partnerships with brands and law enforcement."
— Matthew Bassiur, VP & Head of Alibaba International's Global IP Enforcement Team

The 5-Pillar Strategy

#PillarWhat Specialized Did
1Safety NarrativePublic crush-test videos showing counterfeit helmets/frames failing catastrophically. Every enforcement action framed around consumer safety, not just IP.
2Digital → Physical PipelineOnline monitoring → test purchases → digital evidence from platforms → trace supply chain to factories → law enforcement raids. Don't just play whack-a-mole — follow the chain.
3Platform PartnershipsDirect relationship with Alibaba's IP enforcement team. AI-driven monitoring, rapid takedowns, and joint investigations. 12+ successful offline operations together.
4Legal WarfareUS court judgments for max statutory damages. Even when sellers don't respond, judgments enable domain seizures, payment freezes, and injunctions.
5China TrademarkThe foundation for everything. Without it, enforcement on Chinese soil is nearly impossible.

Recommended Actions for AXIS

Immediate (0–3 months)

  1. Complete China trademark registration — the #1 priority and foundation for all enforcement
  2. Register on Alibaba IPP (IP Protection Platform) with AXIS corporate credentials
  3. Conduct 10–20 test purchases from top counterfeit sellers (GY Sports, Guanyong, etc.)
  4. Document safety failures — test counterfeit foils and create video content showing structural failure
  5. File first batch of IPP complaints targeting worst offenders

Medium-term (3–12 months)

  1. Hire or designate a Brand Protection Manager (even part-time)
  2. Engage Rouse or similar firm for systematic Three-Strike enforcement
  3. File US civil lawsuits against AliExpress sellers
  4. Register IP with China Customs for export interception
  5. Build relationship with Alibaba's IP enforcement team

Long-term (12+ months)

  1. Joint investigation with AliExpress to trace supply chain to Huizhou factories
  2. Coordinate with Chinese law enforcement for factory raids
  3. Record IP with US CBP for import seizures
  4. Publish annual Brand Protection Report for deterrence
